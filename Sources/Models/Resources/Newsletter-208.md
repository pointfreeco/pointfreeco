Some of our recent and most popular work includes:

* [**Isolation**][isolation]

  [isolation]: /collections/beyond-basics/isolation
  
  Our most recent series dedicated to one of the most important and misunderstood parts of modern 
  Swift. We explore isolation from first principles and work through how it shapes correctness,
  architecture, and concurrency in real applications.

* **[Modern persistence][modern-persistence-collection]**

  [modern-persistence-collection]: /collections/modern-persistence

  Our ambitious series exploring what persistence should look like in a modern Swift application,
  from schemas and queries to synchronization, record sharing, and data modeling.

* **[SQLiteData 1.0][sqlite-data-post]**

  [sqlite-data-post]: https://www.pointfree.co/collections/tours/tour-of-sqlitedata

  An alternative to SwiftData built on top of SQLite that supports CloudKit synchronization, 
  data sharing, and embraces value types and makes it easy to test your applications.

* **[DebugSnapshots][debug-snapshots-post]**

  [debug-snapshots-post]: https://www.pointfree.co/blog/posts/207-debugsnapshots-public-beta

  A testing and debugging tool that brings exhaustive testing and laser-focused failure
  messages to reference types, `@Observable` models, and more.

And we have some huge material coming in 2026:

* **Swift ownership**

  We will be taking deep dives into Swift's ownership model, including non-copyable and
  non-escapable types, so that these features can feel practical rather than mysterious.

* **Advanced concurrency in practice**

  We will build [ComposableArchitecture 2.0]'s `Store` type from scratch, giving us an opporunity
  to use every advanced concurrency tool we discussed in our recent [series][isolation]. We will
  show that by designing a library with isolation in mind we can make its tools more testable, 
  easier to understand, and safer to use in concurrent contexts.

* **A new SQLite driver**

  Soon we will unveil a new SQLite driver for Swift that brings an unparalleled suite of testing
  and debugging tools to applications. We think it is going to raise the bar for what Swift 
  developers should expect from persistence tooling.
